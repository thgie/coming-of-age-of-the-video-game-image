Description official descriptions

Somewhere in the galaxy, a huge alien space creature known as Parasitis travels between realms digesting its prey. The latest victim consumed by the alien entity is the planet Abadox. While trying to devour other planets by assuming the form of this planet, the World Alive Force (WAF) launches an all out assault on the alien life form. The player is a single soldier in this military operation.

Abadox: The Deadly Inner War is a side-scrolling shooter. Players must fire upon various enemies which travel across the screen and destroy their bosses as well. One button is used for shooting and another to control the shield ability.

Odd Shooter into the Belly of a Beast

The Good
Decent graphics and memorable gameplay mechanics.

The Bad
While a challenge is occasionally nice, Abadox sometimes tested the limits of frustration. The game function like a pure coin-op. Once those 2 credits are gone, you have to start all over.

The Bottom Line
Abadox was an inventive shooter, providing a difficult challenge and one of the oddest locations for a game. The stages progress through what seem to be the various locations of a giant aliens body.

The game features typical shooter elements -- powerups including speed, shields, and various guns.

Milton Bradley, who published many losers for the NES, had a better game here.

Entertaining Life Force rehash

The Good
Abadox follows the same thrust as the familiar Life Force shooter from Konami. But knowing that does not diminish the fact that this is a fun game in itself. A large, vicious creature wants to snack on your entire planet. It's up to you to travel through the creature's body, fighting all of the bizarre biological creatures within, and make it to the heart to destroy the creature. Along the way, pick up the usual weapon and speed powerups to give you an extra edge over the bio-baddies. Oh yeah, the princess is also inside the creature so you have to find and rescue her as well. That's something they had to lift out of the nominal fantasy adventure game.

The Bad
If one thing frustrated me about Abadox it was the standard issue in the flying/shoot-em-up genre that one hit on your characters makes you go boom.

The Bottom Line
Ignoring the cribbed story elements, Abadox stands on its own as a challenging space shooting game to the bitter end.
